About Us

Paper Bridges



We are the Paper Bridges Chapter at North Carolina. Paper Bridges Corp is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in Maryland that encourage students to write letters to orphanages around the world. We were established in 2017 to support orphans around the world. We strive to provide hope for children in orphanages internationally by writing letters to kids, as well as sending them small gifts to remind them that there are people who care about them. Our letters are written by people of all age groups and walks of life, each full of love and encouragement. We have reached 14 countries, and are continue to make a difference one letter at a time.

Although nothing can replace the critical role parental love constitutes in a child’s life, we can still hope to ease an orphan’s journey by providing encouragement and support. Our members partner and work with school clubs, orphanages, and communities. This year, we aim to expand our networks by partnering with Vietnamese orphanages like the Vinh-Son orphanage. We welcome all to volunteer for our club, but only official members are eligible to receive certificates. We offer service hours to members for writing 100-500 words per letter. If you would like to partner up or have any questions regarding our organization, please submit a response form to us.

How You Can Help

“Not all of us can do great things. But we can do small things with great love.”

Mother Teresa



Just five minutes of your time can show an orphan that they are loved and cared for.



Write Letters

Help us write letters to orphans around the world in orphanages like Orphan's Hope, CASA Guatemala, and Our Little Roses. We will proofread and word check it for you. Be encouraging! Let the orphans know that you are looking out for them. Each letter should be from 100 to 500 words. A few suggestions:

Get them to know you: Where are you from? Share your hobbies or daily activities

Talk about school, sports, friends, or your hidden talents.


Still not sure what to write? Here are some examples

Dear friend, How are you? I hope you find this letter cheer you up. I am looking forward to fall break at my school in east America. It is so cold, so I don't feel like going to school. I am so happy that we don't do a lot today, and are getting ready for the break. I like cooking on the break. I like making hot chocolate with marshmallows. I also like ham for Thanksgiving. What are your favorite foods? Also, if it gets super cold, I could make a snowman in front of my house. I love it also when we have no school when it snows really bad. What do you like to do? I want you to know that I care a lot about you. You are cared, and I enjoy writing these letters.
Dear friend! I am a grandmother living in North Carolina. I am of old age, so I have long forgotten what it is like to be young. However, I love young children and I will tell you this- you have a bright future ahead of you. You are still young, and many hopes and dreams are still ahead of you. I know your dreams may be put on hold right now, due to poverty, discrimination... all sorts of unfair circumstances. I hope and pray for people like you. I wish you find peace in reading this letter. Even though I know that this program is not for old folks like me, but I wish to be a part of a cause that I care about.
