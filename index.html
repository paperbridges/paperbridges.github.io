General FAQs

Why do you need a different website if the main organization already has one?

With the shortening lunch and school schedule, members are asked to follow this website for updates, along with email, Instagram, and any shared documents. Having a website for our state branch will keep all files related to North Carolina state documented in one place. Also, it serves as a portfolio for prospective partners, along with newsletters specifically catered towards our activities. It does not replace the main website, as the main website is also embedded into our state branch’s website.

Is your branch religious?

For clarification, though our branch partners with communities founded upon Christian principles, any contributions to our cause will not be used directly for any religion. Our main goal is to support our mission of supporting children emotionally.

Where does your money come from and what is it for?

Our donation is mostly from members for materials and fees to deliver the letters. However, we are the only branch that donate small cash gifts to our orphanages. Some of it is also covered by a stipend from a Vietnamese American nonprofit won by our president for service excellence. We are allowed for a small grant from the main organization (visit Paper Bridges for more ), but we will request it once we run out of money.

Where do you send cards?

We send cards to partnered organizations and our head organization in Maryland for letter distribution.

Why don't you start helping in America first?

Please visit the Paper Bridges website down below. We've already established partnerships with orphanages in United States. However, we want to expand our mission to further help children in poorer countries that lack basic necessities.
