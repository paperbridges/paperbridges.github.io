About Us

Paper Bridges


We are the Paper Bridges Project at North Carolina. Paper Bridges Corp is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in Maryland that encourage students to write letters to orphanages around the world. We were established in 2017 to support orphans around the world. We strive to provide hope for children in orphanages internationally by writing letters to kids, as well as sending them small gifts to remind them that there are people who care about them. Our letters are written by people of all age groups and walks of life, each full of love and encouragement. We have reached 12 countries, and are continue to make a difference one letter at a time.

Although nothing can replace the critical role parental love constitutes in a child’s life, we can still hope to ease an orphan’s journey by providing encouragement and support. Our members partner and work with with churches, schools, and local communities like the Vietnamese American Association at Raleigh. We welcome all to volunteer for our club, but only official members are eligible to receive certificates and attend missionary trips. We offer service hours to members for writing 100-500 words per letter. If you would like to partner up or have any questions regarding our organization, please submit a response form to us.

How You Can Help

“Not all of us can do great things. But we can do small things with great love.”

Mother Teresa



Just five minutes of your time can show an orphan that they are loved and cared for.



Write Letters

Help us write letters to orphans around the world in orphanages like Orphan's Hope, CASA Guatemala, and Our Little Roses. We will proofread and word check it for you. Be encouraging! Let the orphans know that you are looking out for them. Each letter should be from 100 to 500 words. A few suggestions:

Get them to know you: Where are you from? Share your hobbies or daily activities

Talk about school, sports, friends, or your hidden talents.


Still not sure what to write? Here are some examples

Dear friend, How are you? I hope you find this letter cheer you up. I am looking forward to fall break at my school in east America. It is so cold, so I don't feel like going to school. I am so happy that we don't do a lot today, and are getting ready for the break. I like cooking on the break. I like making hot chocolate with marshmallows. I also like ham for Thanksgiving. What are your favorite foods? Also, if it gets super cold, I could make a snowman in front of my house. I love it also when we have no school when it snows really bad. What do you like to do? I want you to know that I care a lot about you. You are cared, and I enjoy writing these letters.
Dear friend, My name is Vy and i'm currently lived in Raleigh North Carolina. I'm a 16 years old boy but there is nothing special about me, i'm just an average guy who go to school to learn, but mostly to hang out with my friends. To me, friend is someone who play with you, help you, and fixed you when you do something wrong. Sometimes you guy might get in a fight, but that's what friend is all about. They are the one that will always be on your side no matter what, and they are always there when you needed them the most, or when you are in your toughest condition. But friendship won't last forever, there will be time where you guy have to be separate due to various reason like moving, or transferring. Therefore you should be thankful to your friend, those who are always on your side when you needed them the most before it's too late. I'm Vy, and i want to be that friend of your, and i can't wait to shared you more of my opinion, and my everyday experience.
